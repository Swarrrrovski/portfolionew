How I Design

I come at design from the code side — every project below started as a question about how it should feel to play, and the engineering followed from that. Here's the thinking behind the four projects in the gallery.

01
The state machine is the design document
Before writing gameplay code, I map out states and transitions on paper first — hurt, block, attack, death aren't just code states, they're the vocabulary of what a player can feel in a given moment. If a state doesn't change what the player can do or feel, it doesn't belong in the machine.
02
Tune with data, then playtest the tuning
In the endless runner, I didn't guess at difficulty — I logged player performance (survival time, near-misses, death position) and manually adjusted obstacle spacing and enemy speed against that data. It's rule-based tuning rather than a trained model, a limitation I'm upfront about — and exactly why I want graduate-level exposure to more rigorous, data-driven design methods.
03
Protect game feel over feature count
When two-player local input in the fighting game caused timing conflicts, I didn't add more features to compensate — I cut scope and rebuilt input handling until hits registered on-frame. A smaller game that feels right beats a longer feature list that doesn't.
04
Narrative structure is a design constraint, not decoration
Riddle Raaz's chapter pacing changed after early builds felt front-loaded with dialogue before any player agency. I moved narrative payoff to reward correct answers rather than gate them upfront — a small structural choice that changed how the whole game felt to play.

Unity · WebGL Internship

Dynamic Delivery Driver

A browser-based city delivery simulation developed at Vanara Games Studio. Dynamic traffic, timed deliveries, vehicle interactions, and a full scoring system — optimised for WebGL deployment.

  • Traffic systems & delivery objective logic
  • Interactive UI, timers, game-state management
  • Browser optimisation for smooth WebGL play
  • Vehicle interaction & scoring pipeline
✎ design notes
ProblemEarly traffic AI made deliveries feel either trivial or unfair depending on the route — there was no consistent relationship between distance and time pressure.
IterationAdjusted vehicle spawn density and speed variance across several internal builds until timer pressure scaled predictably with delivery distance.
Trade-offChose fewer, better-choreographed vehicles over a visually denser scene, to protect WebGL frame budget — performance constraints shaped the design, not just the code.

Unity · 2D Local Multiplayer

1v1 Fighting Game

A local multiplayer 2D fighting prototype with fully responsive controls, layered combat states, and modular scripting so animations and mechanics plug in cleanly.

  • Run, jump, attack, block, hurt, death states
  • Animation FSM via modular C# scripts
  • Health management & combat interaction system
  • Two-player simultaneous input handling
✎ design notes
ProblemSimultaneous two-player input under Unity's default update loop caused frame-timing conflicts — hits sometimes registered a beat late for one player.
IterationPrototyped three input-polling approaches before settling on a per-player FixedUpdate read, keeping hit detection frame-accurate for both players at once.
Trade-offEarly playtesters said blocking felt "too forgiving" — tightened the block-window timing rather than adding new mechanics to compensate, protecting feel over feature count.

Unity · 2D Story / Quiz

Riddle Raaz

A chapter-based narrative quiz game that blends interactive storytelling with puzzle mechanics. Each chapter unfolds through dialogue systems, progression checkpoints, and question-driven gameplay.

  • Multi-chapter game flow with dialogue engine
  • Question mechanics, transitions & scene management
  • Player engagement via narrative-driven design
  • UI implementation and interaction systems
✎ design notes
ProblemA chapter-based quiz risks collapsing into a repetitive dialogue-then-question loop with no sense of pacing or payoff.
IterationEarly builds felt front-loaded — dialogue before any player agency. Restructured so narrative beats unlock as rewards for correct answers instead of gating content upfront.
Trade-offKept branching shallow by deliberate choice — depth in writing and chapter pacing over branching complexity, given solo-dev scope and timeline.

Endless Runner 2D

A 2D endless runner with procedural obstacle generation, audio management, and a clean modular codebase including Game Manager, Player Controller, and Autocenter scripts.

✎ design notes
ProblemStatic obstacle spacing made the game either too easy to disengage from or unfairly punishing once speed ramped up.
IterationBuilt a dynamic-difficulty layer that logs player performance during a run and manually tunes obstacle spacing and enemy speed against that data.
Trade-offThis is rule-based tuning, not a trained model — a conscious scope decision, and the exact gap I want to close with graduate coursework in applied ML and player modeling.
